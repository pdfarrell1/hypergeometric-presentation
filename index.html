The Scrabble Tile Problem

Scenario: We have 100 Scrabble tiles in a bag.

Key Information:

  • 12 tiles are the letter "E"
  • 88 tiles are other letters
  • We draw 7 tiles without replacement

Question: What's the probability of getting exactly 2 E's?

Why Use Hypergeometric Distribution?

We use the hypergeometric distribution because:

  • We're sampling without replacement
  • We have a finite population (100 tiles)
  • Each tile can be classified as "success" (E) or "failure" (not E)
  • We want exactly k successes in n draws

This is different from binomial distribution, which assumes sampling with replacement.

The Hypergeometric Formula

P(X = k) = [C(K,k) × C(N-K,n-k)] / C(N,n)

Where:

  • N = Total population size (100 tiles)
  • K = Number of success items in population (12 E's)
  • n = Number of draws (7 tiles)
  • k = Number of successes we want (2 E's)
  • C(a,b) = Combination: "a choose b"

Substituting Our Values

P(X = 2) = [C(12,2) × C(88,5)] / C(100,7)

Breaking it down:

  • C(12,2) = Ways to choose 2 E's from 12 E's
  • C(88,5) = Ways to choose 5 non-E's from 88 non-E's
  • C(100,7) = Total ways to choose 7 tiles from 100

Calculating Each Combination

C(12,2) = 12!/(2! × 10!) = (12 × 11)/(2 × 1) = 66

C(88,5) = 88!/(5! × 83!) = 533,175,232

C(100,7) = 100!/(7! × 93!) = 16,007,560,800

The combination formula C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!) gives us the number of ways to choose r items from n items without regard to order.

Final Calculation

P(X = 2) = (66 × 533,175,232) / 16,007,560,800

Numerator: 66 × 533,175,232 = 35,189,565,312

Final Result: 35,189,565,312 / 16,007,560,800 ≈ 0.2198

Answer: 21.98%

There's approximately a 22% chance of getting exactly 2 E's when drawing 7 tiles from our Scrabble bag.
